Case Study

Cookie Cats
A/B Testing

Ran a full statistical analysis on a 90,000-user mobile game experiment — testing whether moving the progression gate from level 30 to level 40 improves player retention, using chi-square tests, Mann-Whitney U, and bootstrap confidence intervals.

Experiment at a Glance

Cookie Cats is a mobile puzzle game where players hit a timed gate at a set level — forcing a wait before they can continue. The experiment tested whether shifting the gate from level 30 (control) to level 40 (treatment) would improve 1-day retention, 7-day retention, and overall game rounds played.

03

Results

Metric Gate 30 (Control) Gate 40 (Treatment) Lift Significance
1-Day Retention 44.82% 44.23% −1.32% Not Sig. p=0.075
7-Day Retention 19.02% 18.20% −4.31% Significant p=0.0016
Avg Game Rounds 52.5 51.3 — Not Sig.

Bootstrap analysis (5,000 iterations) confirmed the 7-day result: the 95% confidence interval for the difference [−1.30 pp, −0.32 pp] excludes zero, ruling out chance. Game rounds were similar across groups, indicating the gate position does not meaningfully change how much players engage before hitting the gate.

04

Key Insights

Hedonic Adaptation
Players who reach the gate earlier (level 30) feel a stronger urge to return after the wait. Those who progress further (level 40) may feel more satisfied — and less motivated to come back.
7-Day Retention Matters Most
Seven-day retention is the strongest predictor of long-term monetisation. A −4.3% relative drop at Gate 40 directly threatens revenue, making this the decisive metric.
1-Day Effect is Negligible
While Gate 30 edges Gate 40 on day-1 retention, the difference is not statistically significant (p=0.075) and Cohen's h ≈ −0.012 shows essentially no practical effect.
Engagement is Unaffected
Median rounds played (17 vs 16) are nearly identical across groups. The gate position changes retention behaviour but not in-session engagement volume.
05

Recommendation

✅
Keep Gate 30 — do not ship Gate 40
Gate 30 wins on the metric that matters most: 7-day retention, with statistical significance confirmed by both chi-square (p=0.0016) and bootstrap CI. Moving the gate later improves nothing and meaningfully hurts long-term player return rates.

Next steps: segment results by platform (iOS / Android), analyse in-app purchase data alongside retention, and consider testing Gate 50 vs Gate 30 to further understand the psychological sweet spot.
